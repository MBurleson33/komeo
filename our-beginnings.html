Our Beginnings

Our ministry was founded in October 2010 with a dedication and purpose for sharing the Gospel and caring for orphans in Sierra Leone, West Africa. We have established a child sponsorship program for more than 100 children and developed partnerships with other non-profit organizations and businesses to leverage support for the orphanages and surrounding villages.

The story behind our name, "Komeo" (pronounced ko-MAY-o), is an interesting one. Komeo is an ancient Greek word meaning "tend to" or "take care of." After choosing the name through much prayer and consideration, we learned that it also carries profound meaning in one of Sierra Leone's African tribal languages. The phrase "kom mio" in Temne means "to give birth to" — it is what orphans cry out to people when asking someone to take care of them, and what people cry out when asking God to be their heavenly Father. We believe our name was God-given.

We work with two orphanages located in Wellington and Waterloo. Many of these children lost their parents during the 12-year civil war waged by rebel forces beginning in 1991 over the country's diamond resources. Peace was declared in 2002, but not before 75,000 people were killed, 20,000 were left as amputees, and 80% of the country's infrastructure was destroyed.

In 1995, during the height of the war, Pastor Hassan Mansaray began taking in children whose parents had been kidnapped or murdered. Before long, he had 64 children in his care and no source of funding — most missionaries and aid groups had fled the country. By faith, Pastor Mansaray persevered and relied on God to provide day-by-day for their needs.

Today, more than 100 children at the orphanage call Pastor Mansaray their father. They are incredible kids who have been raised as Christians in a primarily Muslim country, and they feel called by God to rebuild their nation. They aspire to be bankers, lawyers, economists, government officials, doctors, and professional athletes — but beyond that, they have a heart for ministry and want to share Christ with all who cross their path. They love preaching and leading worship in their church, as well as participating in outreach projects to remote villages.

We are immensely grateful to the Lord for all He has done, and we are blessed to witness His work in the lives of these children. We know that He will continue to do great things through Komeo — and ultimately raise up these kids to be future leaders in their country.

Key Milestones

1991–2002

Sierra Leone Civil War

A brutal 12-year conflict killed 75,000 people and left 80% of the country's infrastructure destroyed — creating a generation of orphans.

1995

Pastor Mansaray Begins

During the height of the war, Pastor Hassan Mansaray begins taking in children whose parents have been killed or kidnapped — relying on God alone to provide.

October 2010

Komeo Is Founded

Komeo International Ministries is established in Oklahoma City with a mission to share the Gospel and care for orphans in Sierra Leone.

2014

Ebola Crisis

The Ebola outbreak devastates local villages and strains resources, but Komeo and its partners continue to provide care and support.

Today

150+ Children in Our Care

Two orphanages in Wellington and Waterloo serve more than 150 children — all raised as Christians with a heart to rebuild their nation.
